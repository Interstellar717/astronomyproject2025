Eclipses

Eclipses are when one celestial body casts a shadow on another while being viewed from another celestial body. There are two types of eclipses that can be viewed by Earth. These are solar eclipses and lunar eclipses. Solar eclipses are when the sun is eclipsed by the moon, meaning that the moon blocks out the sun. Lunar eclipses are when the Earth blocks out the moon from the sun's perspective, so the Earth casts a shadow on the moon. This makes the moon look dark from the Earth's point of view.
